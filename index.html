Dhading District is one of the districts located in the Bagmati Province of Nepal. It is situated in the central part of the country and covers an area of approximately 1,926 square kilometers. The district headquarters is in Dhading Besi, which is about 80 kilometers west of the capital city, Kathmandu. Geographically, Dhading District is quite diverse, ranging from lowland areas along the Trishuli River to high mountain peaks in the northern part. The district is bordered by Nuwakot and Rasuwa districts in the east, Gorkha district in the west, Kathmandu and Nuwakot districts in the south, and Tibet (China) in the north. Dhading is known for its natural beauty and scenic landscapes. The Trishuli River flows through the district, providing opportunities for water-based activities like rafting and fishing. The district is also home to several hills and mountains, including the Ganesh Himal range, which offers trekking and mountaineering opportunities. The majority of the population in Dhading District is engaged in agriculture, with farming being the primary occupation. The district is known for the production of crops such as rice, maize, millet, wheat, and vegetables. Livestock rearing and dairy farming are also common. Dhading has a rich cultural heritage, and various ethnic groups reside in the district, including Brahmin, Chhetri, Tamang, Gurung, Newar, Magar, and others. Each ethnic group has its own unique traditions, languages, and festivals, contributing to the cultural diversity of the region. In terms of infrastructure, Dhading District has seen improvements over the years. Road connectivity has been enhanced, linking the district with major cities and towns. However, some remote areas still lack proper access to transportation and other basic facilities. Overall, Dhading District is a picturesque region with a rich cultural heritage and opportunities for agricultural activities, trekking, and exploring the natural beauty of the Himalayas.
